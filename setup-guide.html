On-site/Hybrid Meetings

For on-site interpreting with professional interpreting consoles (like Bosch), you'll need to connect your device to the console's audio output. This setup allows Sight-Terp Assist to capture the source audio for real-time speech recognition.

Note: Physical cables are necessary for on-site meetings. Investing in the recommended cables and learning proper connections will be worthwhile for professional interpreting work.

Recommended Setup: Mac/PC + JOYO Mixer

Required Equipment:

  • UGREEN male-to-male audio AUX cable (3.5mm)
  • Amazon Basics Gold Plated 6.35mm to 3.5mm adapter
  • JOYO Momix Cab internal recording audio mixer
  • USB-C to USB-C cable (included with JOYO mixer)

Connection Steps:

  1. Connect one end of the UGREEN audio cable to your interpreting console's headphone port
  2. Connect the other end to the 6.35mm Audio Jack Adapter
  3. Plug the adapter into the 6.35mm female port of the JOYO mixer
  4. Connect the mixer to your computer using the USB-C to USB-C cable
  5. Insert your headphones into the mixer and adjust volume using the side buttons
  6. In Sight-Terp Assist, refresh the device list and select "Momix Cab" as the audio source
Why JOYO Mixer?
  • Portable and high-fidelity at a good price
  • No interference when connected to power source
  • USB-powered - won't lower headphone volume
  • Excellent acoustic shock protection
  • Easy volume adjustment with dedicated buttons
  • Converts mono channels to stereo for older consoles

Alternative Device Setups

iPad (Type-C Port)

Required Equipment:

  • Amazon Basics Gold Plated 6.35mm to 3.5mm adapter
  • UGREEN male-to-male audio AUX cable
  • Rode SC4 adapter
  • Belkin USB-C to 3.5mm headset and charger adapter

Connection Steps:

  1. Connect UGREEN cable to 6.5mm to 3.5mm adapter
  2. Plug adapter into Bosch console's 6.5mm port
  3. Connect other end of UGREEN cable to Rode SC4 (black port)
  4. Plug Rode SC4 TRRS end into Belkin adapter's headset jack
  5. Connect USB-C end to your iPad
  6. Disable "Hey Siri" function on your iPad

iPad (Lightning Port)

Required Equipment:

  • Amazon Basics Gold Plated 6.35mm to 3.5mm adapter
  • UGREEN male-to-male audio AUX cable
  • Rode SC4 adapter
  • Belkin RockStar Lightning to 3.5mm Audio Cable + Audio Charger Splitter

Connection Steps:

  1. Follow same steps as Type-C iPad setup
  2. Use Belkin Lightning adapter instead of USB-C adapter
  3. Connect lightning connector to iPad's lightning port
  4. Ensure "Hey Siri" is disabled

iPhone (Lightning Port)

Required Equipment:

  • Amazon Basics Gold Plated 6.35mm to 3.5mm adapter
  • UGREEN male-to-male audio AUX cable
  • Rode SC4 adapter
  • Belkin RockStar Lightning to 3.5mm Audio Cable + Audio Charger Splitter

Connection Steps:

  1. Same setup as Lightning iPad
  2. Disable "Hey Siri" function
  3. System should automatically source audio from connection

Remote Meetings

For remote interpreting, you'll need to capture system audio from your computer. This allows Sight-Terp Assist to process the audio from your video conferencing software.

Mac Setup

Macs with Apple Chip (M1/M2/M3)

Best Solution - System Audio Capture:

  1. Download the desktop version of Sight-Terp Assist
  2. Click the microphone button in the bottom left
  3. Select "System Audio" as the input source
  4. You're all set!

Macs with Intel Chip

Software Solution:

  1. Download and install Sight-Terp Audio Driver (free)
  2. Setup Multi-output Device in Audio MIDI Setup
  3. Select Multi-output Device as system audio output
  4. Select Sight-TerpVirtualCable 2ch as input device in browser settings
  5. Open Sight-Terp Volume Controller to adjust system audio volume
Note: When installing, macOS may show security prompts. Refer to Apple's instructions on "Safely open apps on your Mac" to complete installation.

Hardware Approach for Mac (Advanced Users)

For USB Microphones or Laptop Microphones:

Required Equipment:

  • TRS splitter
  • TRS audio cable
  • USB adapter
  1. Plug TRS splitter into computer's speaker output port
  2. Connect headphones to one female port of splitter
  3. Connect other female port with TRS audio cable
  4. Plug TRS cable into USB adapter's microphone port
  5. Insert USB adapter into computer's USB port
  6. In Sight-Terp Assist, select USB adapter as audio input source
  7. Test headphones and microphone in your video conferencing software

For Headsets or Standalone 3.5mm Microphones:

Required Equipment:

  • TRRS splitter (if computer has integrated audio port)
  • TRS splitter
  • TRS audio cable
  • USB adapter
  1. If computer has integrated audio port, plug TRRS splitter first
  2. Plug TRS splitter into TRRS splitter's headphone port
  3. Follow steps from previous setup
  4. Ensure computer picks up voice from microphone through TRRS splitter

Windows PC Setup

Recommended Solution - System Audio

Direct System Audio Capture:

  1. Download the desktop version of Sight-Terp Assist
  2. Click the microphone button
  3. Select "System Audio" as the input source
  4. You're all set!

Realtek Sound Card Setup

For PCs with Realtek Sound Cards:

  1. Go to Windows Start Menu > PC Settings > System > Sound > Sound control panel
  2. Or press Windows Key + R, input "mmsys.cpl sounds" then press Enter
  3. In Playback tab, select "Speakers (Realtek Audio)" as default playback device
  4. Select this device as audio output on your meeting platform
  5. Go to Recording tab, right-click empty space and select "Show Disabled Devices"
  6. Right-click and enable "Stereo Mix (Realtek Audio)"
  7. Double-check microphone is selected as default device
  8. In Sight-Terp Assist, select "Stereo Mix (Realtek Audio)" as audio source

VB-Cable Solution

Alternative Software Solution:

Required Software:

  • VB-Cable (free)
  • Audio Repeater Pro (paid with free trial)
  1. Download and install VB-Cable and Audio Repeater Pro
  2. Right-click VB-Cable installer > Run as administrator
  3. Restart your computer
  4. Select personal headphones as system audio output device
  5. Open Audio Repeater Pro
  6. Select personal headphones as Input Device (Wave in)
  7. Select Cable Input (VB-Audio Virtual Cable) as Output Device (Wave out)
  8. Click "Start" to send audio into virtual cable
  9. In Sight-Terp Assist, select "Cable Output (VB-Audio Virtual Cable)" as audio source
Note: The free version of Audio Repeater Pro has periodic silence. Try it first before purchasing.

Hardware Approach for PC

For Standalone Microphones:

Required Equipment:

  • TRS splitter
  • TRS audio cable
  • USB adapter
  1. Plug TRS splitter into computer's speaker output port
  2. Connect headphones to one female port
  3. Connect other female port with TRS audio cable
  4. Plug TRS cable into USB adapter's microphone port
  5. Insert USB adapter into computer's USB port
  6. Select USB adapter as audio input source in Sight-Terp Assist
  7. Test headphones and microphone in video conferencing software

Second Device Setup

Using Sight-Terp Assist on a second device is great for separating tasks. You can interpret on your main computer and use Sight-Terp Assist on a tablet or second computer.

Setup Steps:

  1. Use a TRS splitter to send audio to both headphones and second device
  2. Follow the on-site meeting connection method for your second device
  3. This allows you to use touch features and handwriting on tablets
  4. Keep your main computer focused on the virtual meeting

Troubleshooting

Common Issues and Solutions

No Audio Detected

  • Check all cable connections are secure
  • Verify audio source is selected correctly in Sight-Terp Assist
  • Test audio output from your interpreting console
  • Try refreshing the device list in Sight-Terp Assist

Low Volume After Connection

  • This is normal - audio signal is split into two channels
  • Turn up volume on your console
  • Ask technician to increase volume from stage audio mixer
  • Remind speakers to speak closer to microphones

Audio Quality Issues

  • Check for loose connections
  • Try different audio cables
  • Ensure proper grounding (especially with JOYO mixer)
  • Check for interference from other devices

Software Issues

  • Restart Sight-Terp Assist
  • Refresh the webpage if using browser version
  • Reboot your device
  • Check system audio permissions
  • Update audio drivers
Still having issues? If problems persist after trying these solutions, please contact our support team. We're ready to provide one-on-one assistance.
